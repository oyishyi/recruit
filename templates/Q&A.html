Frequently asked questions and answers

Question 1: Can I have more than one job seeker account?
Answer:

You can have multiple accounts, but you’ll need a different email address for each account. Your email address is your username and needs to be unique. Therefore, once you’ve registered with an email address, it can’t be used by anyone else.

If you want to create another account and need a new email address, we recommend registering with a free email service such as Gmail or Hotmail/Outlook.

If I have two or more job seeker accounts with different email addresses, can I merge them into one? Unfortunately, we’re not able to merge your accounts, but if you wish to delete one, you’ll find the instructions here.

Question 2: How do I change my password?
Answer:

To change your existing password:

  1. Sign in to your personal account
  2. Click your icon at the top right-hand side of the page and click Account
  3. Click Reset password
  4. Now enter your Current password (your password must contain 6 characters or more)
  5. Then Enter new password and Confirm password
  6. Finally, click Submit
  7. And that's it, you're done.

Question 3: What’s the best way to search for a job?
Answer:

To start your search, type a Keyword, select a Employment type, enter a range of salary and finally, click the Search Jobs button. See the image below.



The more terms you enter, the more specific your search results will be. Want a broader search and more job ads? Add fewer terms or use general phrases.

Keywords

Type the job or skills you're looking for in the keyword field. Use double quotation marks to use a specific phrase. For example, "junior accountant" will only return job opportunities that contain that phrase.

Question 4: What is a profile?
Answer:

Your Profile includes your personal information, TAFE and university qualifications, licences and other certifications, and is useful as an addition to your application.

Did you know that employers can search and view your profile separate to the apply process? (if you allow this through your profile visibility settings). This means your dream job could come knocking, even when you’re not actively looking for a role.
